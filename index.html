Hello, I am Nimo

I started out as a secondary school teacher in Nakuru teaching Geography and Swahili. I loved the craft of explaining things, breaking down complexity until it clicked. But I always knew I wanted more for myself. It was Remote Sensing, buried inside my Geography degree, that first showed me what technology could do, how data could map the world, tell stories, solve real problems.

That curiosity didn't go away. It grew. So I made the leap.

A year into networking and I have done OLTs configurations, set up PPPoE, static and hotspot deployments, and found joy documenting every step clearly, so anyone can follow. I work as a L1 tech-support but I show up to every project like an engineer, because that's what I'm building towards.

My goal is to bridge a gap I've seen on both sides: most engineers can't explain their work, and most educators don't understand the infrastructure. I intend to be both. A network engineer who can teach, design, and communicate with the clarity this industry badly needs.

Based in Nairobi, Kenya. Building in public.
